The Man Who Lived Is The New President

Donald Trump began his political comeback when many in his own party wanted him to go away. He announced his third White House bid days after Republicans underwhelmed in the 2022 midterm elections, a performance that prominent GOP figures laid squarely at his feet — for the candidates he supported, for lingering resentments over the January 6, 2021, attack on the US Capitol and for his unwillingness to recede from public life in the aftermath of defeat. Surrounded at Mar-a-Lago by the close allies and aides that had yet to abandon him during his post-presidency exile, Trump assigned blame elsewhere, including the justice system that had raided his Palm Beach estate three months prior. He offered a dark assessment of the country after he left office and forecasted that before long voters would turn against those in charge. “I have no doubt that by 2024, it will sadly be much worse, and they will see clearly what has happened and is happening to our country,” Trump said, “and the voting will be much different.” By early Wednesday morning, Trump’s prediction had materialized. Millions of Americans, including pivotal voters in Midwest and Sun Belt battlegrounds, cast ballots that clinched Trump’s historic comeback — one that promises to reshape American politics for the foreseeable future...

The Unanswered Chronicles of Artyom Shvetsov in ITMO’s Frontend Chat

In the fast-paced realm of ITMO’s frontend chat, everyone has questions, but few ask them as persistently as Artyom Shvetsov. Known for his knack for detail and a seemingly endless well of curiosity, Artyom fires off questions like a well-oiled machine. Will anyone answer? Sometimes the silence speaks louder than words. Each question from Artyom appears like a little island in the chat, surrounded by waves of other students’ urgent exchanges and memes. His inquiries about project specifics, class decisions, and code details float by — unanswered, like philosophical riddles in a very busy inbox. Every so often, the group’s mentor, Oleg Mokhov, does notice a question and lends his guidance, like a lighthouse in the storm. But for the most part, Artyom’s queries quietly collect, forming a growing archive of “great questions left behind.” Through it all, Artyom keeps up his questioning, perhaps to one day receive an answer — or at least give everyone something to think about. His ignored questions have become a fixture, an unintentional comic relief in the intense landscape of ITMO’s frontend chat.
